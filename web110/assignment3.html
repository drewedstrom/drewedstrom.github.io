Assignment 3

Email Activities:

I chose to do the tutorials which cover the basic components of emails (activity 1). Though I was familiar with much of what was covered, I finally learned the difference between BCC and CC. Both are used to add recipients to an email, but using BCC keeps the recipient(s) hidden from other recipients. I can see this being especially useful in a job setting where I need to copy someone on to observe a problematic email chain without alerting the other member(s).

I chose to do the tutorial on sending, receiving, and responding to email (activity 2). For this activity, I opted to focus on the Gmail tutorial. I did this for two reasons: First, it has been my email service of choice for quite some time. Second, I was curious as to if I could find any features I wasn’t previously familiar with. Ultimately, I learned how to add a signature to my emails. To do this, I just had to go into the settings tab of Gmail, select the signature tab, and then format my signature to my liking. I see this being the most useful when it comes to my professional life, since I can make it so my name, job title, and necessary contact information are always appended to the end of emails. This will ensure it is clear to the recipient(s) who I am and provide a means for further contact between us.

Social Media Activity:

I chose to do the LinkedIn tutorial as part of the exploration of social medias activity (activity 2). My intention here was to see if there were any aspects of the platform I was not familiar with. Ultimately, I found that I have been missing out on the social networking side, especially when it comes to the Groups feature. These are communities that provide networking opportunities and share information relating to that group. For instance, you could have an IT group that shares an article detailing how AI is changing the nature of the industry. I can see this feature providing me with information which will enable me to both continue performing my job effectively and adapt to a constantly evolving environment.

Communications Activity:

I chose to read up on the legal problems associated with file sharing (activity 5). Interestingly, I learned that I can rip my music CDs to my online music collection—so long as they are just available to me. For the longest time, I had been uncertain about doing this because I was concerned about the prospect of violating copyright law. But now that I know that it’s acceptable for me to do, I can finally digitize what remains of my physical music collection.
