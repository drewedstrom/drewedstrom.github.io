Assignment 2a

The first activity I did was practicing using web browsers (activity 3). In this activity, I was provided with tutorials for using Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Opera. For my purposes, I chose to focus on Opera as I am very familiar with the rest of these browsers. What I ultimately found was that Opera is quite similar to really any other browser out there, although it does tout the integration of commonly used messenger services. I can’t say as I have seen that in other browsers I’ve used. Now, as I’m somebody who usually installs the standalone apps for these services, I have my doubts regarding how useful this feature will be to me. That said, I suppose if I do require this feature in the future, I know I can use Opera.

The other activity I chose was configuring browser settings (activity 4). As you can probably guess, this activity asks that you dig through browser settings to familiarize yourself with what your options for customizations are. For my purposes, I chose to focus on Brave browser since it is the one that I regularly use. When everything is all said and done, there were two settings I discovered that stood out to me: the first was the ability to disable Brave’s AI assistant Leo for address bar suggestions. I am personally not big on all of this AI integration junk companies are littering their browsers with, so I am thrilled at any opportunity to limit its intrusions. The other setting I found was a prompt on 404 pages to use the archived version from the wayback machine. Now this is especially useful because if a particular webpage is no longer available, the browser can redirect you to an archived version with just the click of a button. This saves the user the hassle of having to visit the wayback machine and enter the URL themselves (although this is admittedly not that hard). With these two settings at my disposal, I am able to engage in a more interactive search experience than one plagued by AI as well as save myself time when I need to view archived webpages.
