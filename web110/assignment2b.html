The first activity I chose to do was comparing search engines (activity 2). In this comparison, I looked at the Google, Bing, and Yahoo search engines. Notably, this also was supposed to include the Ask search engine, however, it was discontinued earlier this year. What I observed is that of the three search engines I listed, Google provided me with the most relevant results. For instance, when I searched for web search engines, Google immediately gave me direct links to alternate search engines such as Brave, DuckDuckGo, and Bing. The same query when used with Yahoo and Bing simply gave me websites that list various search engines. While these are still relevant to my search, I feel this approach isn’t quite as direct as what Google provided me with. That likely explains why Google has been my search engine of choice for, well, most of my life—and will continue to be until something better comes along. With that being said, I will continue to do as I have always done, which is switch between search engines when the one I’m using isn’t quite giving me what I need. As I explained, they can and do yield different results from one another. It’s best to think of them as tools—sometimes you just need a different one for the job.

I will say that I was already aware of the existence of quotes and Boolean expressions to aid in finding more relevant information, though I was not familiar with the site: operator.
